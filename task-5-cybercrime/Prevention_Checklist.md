#  Stay Safe Online: A Student’s Guide to Cyber-Security

As a Student Digital Ambassador, maintaining high standards of cyber hygiene is essential. Below is a critical checklist for navigating the digital landscape safely.

---

###  Financial & UPI Safety
* **The UPI 'Golden Rule':** **NEVER** enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. A PIN is strictly required only to *send* or *pay* money.
* **Verify UPI IDs:** Before hitting 'Send', always double-check the recipient's name and UPI ID in the app to avoid 'Wrong Transfer' scams or spoofed accounts.

###  Reporting Channels
* **The 1930 Helpline:** Memorize the national helpline number **1930**. Reporting financial fraud within the first **"Golden Hour"** significantly increases the chances of authorities freezing the scammer's bank account.
* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** Always report any cyber-harassment, identity theft, or financial fraud at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in).

### Account & Network Privacy
* **WhatsApp Privacy:** Set your 'Profile Photo' and 'About' to **'My Contacts'** only. This prevents scammers from stealing your photo to impersonate you and ask your friends for emergency funds.
* **Public Wi-Fi Hygiene:** Never log into the VIT Student Portal or banking apps while connected to free public Wi-Fi (e.g., at railway stations or cafes). Use a **VPN** or your mobile data instead.
* **HTTPS Check:** Before entering your registration number or password on any site, ensure the URL starts with `https://` and displays the **padlock icon** in the address bar.
* **2-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Enable 2FA on your Gmail, LinkedIn, and Instagram. This ensures that even if a password is compromised, attackers cannot gain access without the secondary OTP from your device.
